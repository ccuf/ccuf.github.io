Events

The list of past, ongoing, and future events

We have in-person and virtual events. We also have past, ongoing, and planned events. See the gallery of out past events below. But first the list of ongoing and planned programs, which is always work-in-progress. We plan to organize more programs based on CCUF members' interest. If you would like to join one or more of these programs, or volunteer to run your own please get in touch. CCUF is a centre for members by members. We rely on your participation.

Name Status Description Time Place
Academic Office Hours Ongoing (since Oct. 2022) Nodir Kodirov answers your questions about higher education in Canada and elsewhere. It is in AMA (Ask Me Anything) format, which includes college/university preparation, life at graduate school, and beyond. Every 3 month, on the 3rd Wednesday of the month, 8pm-9pm, Vancouver time. In 2023, it is on May 17, Aug. 16, Nov. 15. Virtual, Live Streamed on our Telegram channel.
Immigration Office Hours Ongoing (since March 2023) Makhbuba Ergasheva answers your questions about study permits, work permits, and immigration to Canada. It is in AMA format, but limited to general questions, not case specific. Every 3 month, on the 3rd Wednesday of the month, 8pm-9pm, Vancouver time. In 2023, it is on March 15, June 21, Sep. 20, Dec. 20. Virtual, Live Streamed on our Telegram channel.
Real Estate Office Hours Ongoing (since April 2023) Ella Tumakova answers your questions about renting, buying, and general housing in Canada. It is in AMA format, but limited to general questions, not case specific. Every 3 month, on the 3rd Wednesday of the month, 8pm-9pm, Vancouver time. In 2023, it is on April 19, July 19, Oct. 18. Virtual, Live Streamed on our Telegram channel.
Digital Office Literacy Planned We can cover advanced use of Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets, Power Point and Google Slides, photo and video editing software, and etc. This is for all ages, including K-12 students. Ideally, biweekly or more frequently Probably virtual
Computer Programming Planned At CCUF, we have members employed by world's top tech companies. Some of them are willing to teach computer programming skills, even if they are K-12 students. Ideally, biweekly or more frequently Probably virtual
